Coal is mixed with hot air in a power station.
Water is sent through pipes in the power station.
The water pipes go through a firebox in the power station.
The coal is put in the air in the firebox.
The water heats up so Water very hot.
The water turns into steam.
The steam is pushed out of the pipes into a separate area.
The steam powers turbines.
Magnets in the turbines spin in wire coils.
Electricity is produced.
