Acid rain goes into streams, lakes, and wetlands.
Acid rain makes streams, lakes, and wetlands acidic.
The water absorbs aluminum from soil.
The water becomes toxic to aquatic animals.
Acid rain disrupts the food chain.
Acid rain removes nutrients from soil.
Released aluminum makes it hard for trees to get water from the soil.
Some plants are less able to survive cold temperatures.
Trees have more difficulty reproducing
