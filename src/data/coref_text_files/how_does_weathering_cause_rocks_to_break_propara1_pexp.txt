Mechanical weathering is when rock breaks apart into smaller pieces.
Ice wedges into cracks in rock when water in the rocks freezes, causing breaks in the rocks.
Wetting and drying breaks up rocks that contain clay.
Wet clay expands, putting pressure on other rocks, causing other rocks, to break.
Plant roots break up rocks when Plant roots grow.
Animals dig into soil, helping water to reach down to the bedrock and cause the bedrock to weather.
