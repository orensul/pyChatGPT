Acid rain makes water acidic.
Absorbs aluminum from the soil.
Makes the water toxic to crayfish, clams, fish, and other aquatic animals.
Acid Rain damages forests.
Robs the soil of essential nutrients and releases aluminum in the soil.
Makes it hard for trees to take up water.
Combined with other environmental stressors, the trees are less able to withstand cold temperatures, insects, and disease.
May keep a tree from growing other trees.
