Food enters the body.
The food is broken down.
Sugars from the food enter the bloodstream.
Blood sugar must be within a particular range to avoid damaging many organs.
Sometimes the brain tells the pancreas the blood sugar (glucose) is too low.
The pancreas releases glucagon to change the blood sugar to a better level.
Sometimes the brain tells the pancreas the blood sugar (glucose) is too high.
The pancreas releases insulin to change the blood sugar to a better level.
